1. The Problem We Are Solving

Today, every product on Chem Connect follows the same fulfillment pipeline:

Current flow: Buyer pays through Stripe → MacShip quotes the freight → Purchase order is sent to a Chem Connect warehouse → Warehouse dispatches the goods → MacShip provides scan-based tracking back to the buyer.

This works well for products you stock and dispatch yourself, but it does not work for AdBlue. The reason is simple:

  • AdBlue is supplied by an external company, not Chem Connect.
  • That company runs its own freight network across Australia and uses a pump-in service - a tanker truck pumps the product directly into the buyer's on-site tank, priced per litre by distance.
  • MacShip cannot quote or move this kind of delivery, and the supplier already handles dispatch through their own depots.
  • The supplier needs to know where the order is going, and the buyer needs to know when it will arrive - but no part of that conversation goes through Chem Connect today.

What we need: a second fulfillment path inside Chem Connect, where the supplier acts almost like one of our own warehouses, but with their own freight rules, their own dispatch contact, and a small dashboard so they can keep the buyer informed.

3. The Feature, In Plain English

This work introduces three new ideas to Chem Connect. None of them replace what already exists - they sit alongside the current MacShip-based flow.

3.1 Supplier-managed warehouses

A warehouse can now be marked as supplier-managed. From a Chem Connect admin point of view, supplier-managed warehouses look similar to normal warehouses, but they have two key differences:

  • They have their own login - the supplier can sign in and see only their orders.
  • They have their own freight rules - we do not call MacShip for products fulfilled from a supplier-managed warehouse.

3.2 A second freight strategy

Each product is now linked to one of two freight strategies:

  • MacShip freight (the current default) - applies to every existing product. No change.
  • Supplier-managed freight (new) - applies to AdBlue and any future supplier-fulfilled product. The shipping price at checkout is calculated from the supplier's distance-based rate sheet, not from MacShip.

The buyer does not see this distinction. They get a single shipping price on checkout the same way they do today.

3.3 A small supplier dashboard

The supplier gets a stripped-down version of the Chem Connect admin, showing only the orders that contain their products. From there they can:

  • See incoming orders and the buyer's delivery address.
  • See the answers to the site-access questions (truck access, forklift, etc.).
  • Enter or update an estimated delivery date.
  • Add internal dispatch notes (for example: "leaving Melbourne depot Tuesday").
  • Mark the order as dispatched or delivered.

4. Side-By-Side: Current Flow vs New Flow

This section compares how a normal Chem Connect order works today versus how an AdBlue order will work after this feature is built.

Current MacShip product (e.g. Green Acid Replacement)

  1. Buyer adds product to cart and goes to checkout.
  2. MacShip is called to quote freight.
  3. Buyer pays Chem Connect via Stripe.
  4. Purchase order is sent to one of our warehouses.
  5. Warehouse staff dispatches the order.
  6. MacShip provides tracking; buyer sees scan updates.

New Supplier-managed product (e.g. AdBlue)

  1. Buyer adds product to cart and goes to checkout.
  2. Checkout shows extra site-access questions (truck, forklift).
  3. Freight is quoted from the supplier's distance rate sheet, not MacShip.
  4. Buyer pays Chem Connect via Stripe (unchanged).
  5. Purchase order is sent to the supplier's contact, not a Chem Connect warehouse.
  6. Supplier logs into their dashboard, enters the estimated delivery date and dispatch notes.
  7. Buyer sees the ETA on their order page and receives an email when the supplier sets or updates it.
Note on tracking: Because we do not have MacShip in this flow, the supplier-managed flow does not have real-time scan-based tracking. Instead the buyer sees the supplier's stated estimated delivery date, plus any updates the supplier types in. You confirmed this is acceptable on the call.

5. The End-To-End Order Journey

Step-by-step, this is what an AdBlue order will look like once the feature is live.

1

Buyer browses Chem Connect and adds AdBlue to their cart.

Same product page experience as today - same images, descriptions, AUD pricing. No visible change.

2

Buyer enters their delivery postcode and address at checkout.

Our system recognises that AdBlue is supplier-managed, looks up the buyer's distance from the supplier's freight origin, and quotes the per-litre freight cost from the supplier's rate table. MacShip is not called.

3

Checkout shows site-access questions for AdBlue.

Because the AdBlue tanker is large, the buyer is asked questions like "Can a 19-metre truck access the delivery site?" and "Is a forklift available on site?". These questions only show up when the cart contains a product that requires them. Other products are unaffected.

4

Buyer pays through Stripe, exactly as today.

The money lands in the Chem Connect Stripe account. No split payment, no marketplace mechanic. You reconcile with the supplier later through Xero, the same way you handle any other vendor invoice.

5

Purchase order is routed to the supplier instead of a Chem Connect warehouse.

The supplier's nominated contact receives an email with the order details: products, quantities, buyer's delivery address, the answers to the site-access questions, and any special notes. The email also contains a deep link straight to that order inside the supplier dashboard.

6

Supplier logs into the Chem Connect supplier dashboard.

They see only their own orders. They open the order, review the address and site-access answers, decide which depot it will dispatch from, and type in an estimated delivery date and any dispatch notes.

7

Buyer is automatically notified.

As soon as the supplier saves the estimated delivery date, the buyer receives an email update and the date appears on their Chem Connect order page. If the supplier updates it later (for example, due to a delay), the buyer is notified again.

8

Supplier marks the order as dispatched, then delivered.

Each status change is reflected on the buyer's order page. The order is closed in Chem Connect once it is marked delivered.

9

You reconcile with the supplier in Xero.

Outside the platform, the same way you do today. The Chem Connect order record holds all the figures you need - product cost, freight collected, supplier identity - so the Xero entry is straightforward.

7. The Freight Rate Sheet

You shared a distance-based rate sheet that drives the freight cost for AdBlue. The structure is straightforward:

  • Distance brackets in 100km steps (0-100, 101-200, 201-300, ... up to 4000+ km).
  • For each bracket, a price (either per-litre for bulk, or a fixed amount for packed/IBC).
  • Multiple columns for "bulk", "pack existing", "pack new" and "pre-/post-July rates".

For now we will load only the column you confirm we should use, and ignore the rest until you tell us otherwise. The system will be built so adding extra columns or new rate cards later is a configuration change, not a redevelopment.

Why this matters in the brief: So you know the freight calculation is fully data-driven. If the supplier hands you an updated rate sheet next quarter, we update the configuration; we do not rewrite the feature.

8. Things This Feature Does Not Try To Do

To keep the scope clean and avoid surprises, here is what is explicitly not part of this feature.

  • Real-time scan tracking. The supplier does not have a scanning network. Tracking means "an estimated delivery date the supplier types in", not GPS or barcode scans.
  • Marketplace-style payment splits. Buyer still pays Chem Connect. We do not split the Stripe payment between Chem Connect and the supplier; reconciliation stays in Xero.
  • Letting suppliers create or edit products. You decide which products go on the storefront, the same as today. Suppliers only see and act on their orders.
  • Replacing MacShip. Every existing product continues to use MacShip exactly as it does today. The new path is in addition to MacShip, not instead of it.
  • Cancelling or refunding orders from the supplier dashboard. Refunds stay with you in Stripe / the main admin.

10. Why This Approach Is Worth Building

  • Unblocks AdBlue revenue. Right now AdBlue is "coming soon" on the storefront because the platform cannot fulfill it. This feature flips it live.
  • Keeps your existing business untouched. Every current product, every current warehouse, every current MacShip flow keeps working exactly as it does today. There is no migration risk.
  • Built once, reused for every future supplier. Adding the next supplier (or the one after that) is a configuration job, not a development job - so the cost of growing the supplier network drops sharply after this first build.
  • Buyers stay on Chem Connect for the whole journey. They place the order, see the ETA, get the updates, all from one place - even though the goods physically come from a third party.
  • Suppliers do not need to learn another tool. A small focused dashboard is far easier than asking them to use Chem Connect's full admin or a separate freight portal.
