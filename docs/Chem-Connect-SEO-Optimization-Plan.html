SEO Optimization Plan

Prepared by Sohaib | April 2026

Document Purpose: This document outlines a complete, marketplace-focused SEO roadmap for Chem Connect - Australia’s B2B marketplace for industrial chemicals. It covers the on-page, technical, content, and local SEO improvements every serious chemicals marketplace should have in place to rank in Google, attract qualified buyers, and convert them into customers.

1. Meta Tags & Title Optimization

Goal: Help Google and buyers instantly understand what Chem Connect offers on every page.

What we’ll add / improve

  • Keyword-rich title tags (55-60 chars) unique to each page:
    • Home - “Chem Connect | B2B Chemical Marketplace Australia - Buy Industrial Chemicals Online”
    • Product - “{Product Name} - {CAS No.} | Buy in Bulk Australia | Chem Connect”
    • Category - “Solvents Suppliers Australia | Buy Industrial Solvents Online - Chem Connect”
  • Unique meta descriptions (150-160 chars) with a buyer-focused call to action.
  • Meta keywords covering chemical names, CAS numbers, grades, and common use cases.
  • Canonical URLs on every page - prevents duplicate-content issues from cart, sort, and filter parameters.
  • Robots meta: index,follow on public pages; noindex on cart, checkout, account, and admin routes.
What you get
  • A professional, click-worthy listing in Google instead of a blank or generic one.
  • Higher click-through rate from search results - more visitors for the same ranking.
  • No ranking penalty from duplicate cart/filter URLs being indexed.

2. Open Graph & Social Tags

Goal: Clean, branded previews whenever a product or page is shared on LinkedIn, WhatsApp, email, or Slack - where B2B buyers actually share links.

  • og:title, og:description, og:image, og:url, og:type, og:site_name on every page.
  • twitter:card (summary_large_image), twitter:title, twitter:description, twitter:image.
  • Per-product OG images - product photo + brand watermark.
What you get
  • Every shared link looks polished and trustworthy - no broken or missing previews.
  • Free brand exposure every time a buyer shares a product over WhatsApp, LinkedIn, or email.
  • Higher reply and click rates on outbound sales / marketing emails.

3. Structured Data (JSON-LD Schema)

Goal: Unlock rich results in Google - price, stock, reviews, breadcrumbs, sitelinks search box.

Organization

Chem Connect name, logo, ABN, contact details, social profiles, support email & phone.

WebSite

With SearchAction so Google can render a sitelinks search box for branded queries.

Product

On every product page: name, SKU, brand, image, price, AUD currency, availability, GTIN/CAS, rating, reviews.

Offer / AggregateOffer

Price ranges across pack sizes (500 mL, 1 L, 5 L, 20 L, 200 L drum, IBC).

BreadcrumbList

Home → Category → Sub-category → Product.

LocalBusiness

Warehouse / dispatch locations with service area, hours, phone.

FAQPage

For product Q&A, compliance, shipping, and SDS pages.

Article / BlogPosting

For knowledge-hub and blog content.
What you get
  • Rich Google listings showing price, stock status, star ratings, and breadcrumbs - takes up more screen space than competitors.
  • Eligibility for Google’s Shopping / Merchant free listings.
  • A sitelinks search box for branded searches - buyers search your catalog straight from Google.
  • Typically a 20-40% click-through boost on pages with rich results vs plain listings.

4. Heading Structure & Keyword Optimization

  • One H1 per page, targeting the primary keyword. Home: “B2B Chemical Marketplace for Australian Businesses”.
  • H2 / H3 hierarchy targeting secondary keywords - e.g. “Bulk Chemical Suppliers in Sydney / Melbourne / Brisbane / Perth”, “Laboratory Grade vs Industrial Grade”, “Safety & Compliance (SDS, GHS, Dangerous Goods)”.
  • Natural keyword placement - no stuffing - across hero, feature blocks, and footer.
What you get
  • Google immediately understands what each page is about - faster and stronger ranking.
  • Content stays readable and on-brand for human buyers.
  • Every page becomes an opportunity to rank for a different buyer search.

5. URL Structure & Information Architecture

  • Clean, human-readable URLs: /products/solvents/acetone-99-5-technical-grade.
  • Filter / sort URLs canonicalised back to the clean category URL.
  • Category landing pages for every major chemical family - Solvents, Acids, Bases, Polymers, Surfactants, Reagents, Lab Chemicals, Cleaning Chemicals, Agrochemicals, etc.
  • Breadcrumbs on every product and category page (visible + schema).
What you get
  • URLs buyers can read and trust - higher click-through from search and email.
  • Category hub pages that can rank on their own (e.g. “buy solvents Australia”).
  • A scalable structure as the catalog grows to thousands of products.

6. Sitemap & Robots.txt

  • Dynamic sitemap.xml generated from the database - updates automatically as products are added or removed.
  • Sitemap index split into products-sitemap.xml, categories-sitemap.xml, content-sitemap.xml.
  • robots.txt allowing public pages, disallowing /cart, /checkout, /account, /admin, /api, and linking to the sitemap.
  • Submit sitemap to Google Search Console and Bing Webmaster Tools.
What you get
  • Every new product is discovered and indexed by Google automatically - no manual work.
  • Full visibility into which pages are indexed, which aren’t, and why.
  • Private pages (cart, checkout, admin) stay out of Google permanently.

7. Technical SEO & Core Web Vitals

Ranking factor: Google uses Core Web Vitals as a direct ranking signal. Target - LCP < 2.5s, INP < 200ms, CLS < 0.1 (green on mobile).
  • Image optimization - Next.js <Image> everywhere, WebP / AVIF, lazy-loading below the fold, explicit width/height to prevent layout shift.
  • Font loading - next/font with display: swap; avoid blocking web fonts.
  • Preconnect / preload hints for CDN, Supabase, and image domains.
  • HTTPS everywhere, HTTP → HTTPS redirects, HSTS.
  • Mobile-first responsive - buyers browse SDS and re-order from the field.
  • Helpful 404 / 500 pages (search box + top categories + support link), not dead ends.
  • Automated broken-link check before each release.
What you get
  • A fast site that feels premium - buyers don’t bounce waiting for pages to load.
  • Direct ranking boost from passing Google’s Core Web Vitals thresholds.
  • Higher conversion rates - every 100ms faster measurably lifts B2B conversions.
  • Mobile buyers (site engineers, field teams) can browse and re-order without friction.

8. Image & Media Optimization

  • Descriptive, keyword-rich alt text on every product image - e.g. “Acetone 99.5% technical grade, 20 L HDPE drum - Chem Connect Australia”.
  • Image file names should match the product - not IMG_2938.jpg.
  • Image sitemap entries for product photos so they appear in Google Images - a surprisingly strong source of B2B discovery.
  • Lazy-load non-critical images; eager-load the LCP image on each route.
What you get
  • Products start appearing in Google Images - a channel most B2B competitors ignore.
  • Better accessibility and a stronger overall on-page SEO score.
  • Faster page loads on mobile without sacrificing image quality.

9. Local & Australia-Specific SEO

Why it matters: Australia-specific signals are a huge moat against international chemical sites. Getting this right is the fastest route to ranking for high-intent local queries.
  • Google Business Profile for the head office and each warehouse - fully filled in (categories, hours, photos, services, Q&A).
  • NAP consistency (Name, Address, Phone) across the site footer, Contact page, GBP, and all Australian directories.
  • AU business directories: Yellow Pages AU, True Local, Hotfrog, Australian Business Register, plus industry directories.
  • Location landing pages: “Chemical Supplier Sydney”, “Bulk Chemicals Melbourne”, “Industrial Chemicals Brisbane”, “Perth Chemical Distributor” - with unique content, delivery info, and local contact.
  • Language / region tags: lang="en-AU", hreflang="en-AU", currency locked to AUD, GST-inclusive pricing where required.
  • Dangerous Goods / freight messaging per state - trust + SEO signal.
What you get
  • A strong moat against overseas chemical sites competing for Australian queries.
  • Appearance in Google Maps and the “Local Pack” for city-based searches.
  • Inbound enquiries from buyers who filter for Australian / GST-registered suppliers.
  • A landing page that can rank in every major city you ship to.

10. Content Marketing & Topical Authority

Goal: B2B buyers research before they buy. We want Chem Connect to be the answer at every stage of that research.

Knowledge hub content pillars

  • How-to & buyer guides - “How to choose industrial-grade acetone”, “IBC vs drum - which is right for your operation?”
  • Compliance content - Australian SDS library, GHS labelling, ADG Code basics, SUSMP scheduling.
  • Application pages per industry - mining, agriculture, food & beverage, water treatment, pharma, cleaning, manufacturing, laboratory, construction.
  • CAS / product encyclopedia - one indexable page per chemical with properties, uses, hazards, and a “Buy on Chem Connect” CTA. This is the single biggest long-tail traffic lever for a chemicals marketplace.
  • Comparison pages - grade A vs grade B, supplier A vs supplier B.
  • Case studies and customer stories (with schema).
Suggested cadence: 2 high-quality articles per week to start. Scale up once content-to-conversion data is visible in GA4.
What you get
  • Chem Connect becomes the trusted reference for Australian chemical buyers - not just another shop.
  • Ranking for hundreds of long-tail queries that never run out - compounding free traffic.
  • A captive audience for retargeting, email capture, and account signups.
  • Natural backlinks as other industry sites cite your guides and SDS library.

11. Product Page SEO (the highest-ROI pages)

  • Unique, non-boilerplate description (300+ words) - never copy the manufacturer blurb verbatim.
  • Key specs table: CAS No., purity, grade, appearance, density, pack sizes, UN number, hazard class.
  • Pack-size / pricing matrix (already part of the platform) with AUD pricing and stock indicators.
  • Downloadable SDS (PDF) with descriptive filename and anchor text.
  • Shipping & lead-time info per state (ties into MacShip integration).
  • FAQs with FAQ schema - minimum order, lead time, bulk pricing, dangerous goods handling.
  • Related products & “Customers also bought” - boosts internal linking + crawl depth.
  • Ratings & reviews - essential for AggregateRating rich results.
What you get
  • Product pages that rank on their own - each one a standalone sales channel.
  • Higher add-to-cart and quote-request rates from buyers who get all specs in one place.
  • Fewer pre-sale support questions because the page already answers them.
  • Internal linking that spreads ranking power across the whole catalog.

12. Trust, Authority & Backlinks

  • Backlinks from Australian industry bodies: RACI, IChemE AU, Chemistry Australia, NSW EPA, local chambers of commerce.
  • Guest posts on manufacturing, mining, and laboratory publications.
  • Supplier / brand pages - each supplier gets a branded page; suppliers will naturally link back.
  • PR - product launches, new supplier onboarding, sustainability initiatives.
  • On-site trust signals: ABN, compliance badges, payment logos, customer logos, testimonials, dangerous-goods licences.
What you get
  • Higher Google authority - the single biggest long-term ranking factor.
  • Direct referral traffic from industry sites, directories, and press mentions.
  • Immediate buyer trust the first time they land on the site.

13. Analytics, Tracking & Search Console

  • Google Search Console - verified, sitemap submitted, weekly monitoring of coverage, CWV, and top queries.
  • Google Analytics 4 with e-commerce events (view_item, add_to_cart, begin_checkout, purchase).
  • Bing Webmaster Tools - still relevant for corporate buyers on Edge.
  • Conversion tracking for quote requests, signups, and orders.
  • Rank tracking for a curated list of ~100 target keywords.
What you get
  • Clear visibility into what’s working - which keywords, pages, and products drive revenue.
  • Data-backed decisions on where to invest in content, ads, and new categories.
  • Early warning if traffic drops, so issues can be fixed before revenue is affected.

14. Security, Performance & Crawl Budget

  • HTTPS + HSTS and security headers (X-Content-Type-Options, Referrer-Policy, Permissions-Policy).
  • Cache & CDN for static assets and product images.
  • Pagination semantics on product listings, with canonical rules on filter combinations to protect crawl budget.
  • Faceted navigation rules - avoid creating infinite filter URLs in the index.
What you get
  • Google spends its crawl budget on real products - not on junk URL combinations.
  • A secure, padlocked site that passes enterprise-buyer security reviews.
  • Stable performance and rankings even as the catalog scales to thousands of SKUs.

15. Accessibility (WCAG) - A Genuine SEO Win

Google increasingly rewards accessible sites, and accessibility overlaps heavily with SEO.

  • Semantic HTML, ARIA where needed, keyboard navigation, colour contrast, focus states, alt text, and form labels.
  • Accessible PDFs for SDS documents where practical.
What you get
  • A larger addressable audience - including buyers using screen readers or keyboard navigation.
  • Reduced legal risk from Australian accessibility and disability-discrimination obligations.
  • A secondary ranking boost - Google rewards well-structured, accessible HTML.
