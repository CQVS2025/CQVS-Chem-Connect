Customer Reviews — Scope Additions for Sohaib

Addendum to the Customer Reviews Feature proposal

About this document: A focused list of items to add to scope before Sohaib starts building the Customer Reviews feature, plus a short list of confirmations needed from him to size the work properly. Each item exists for a specific reason — SEO, ACCC compliance, abuse prevention, or operational reality — noted inline.

1. Must-add to scope before he builds

Eight items. None are optional — each closes a real gap in the original proposal that would surface later either as an ACCC risk, a Google rich-result no-show, an abuse vector, or a product-page that’s broken on day one.

1 Minimum review threshold for AggregateRating schema

Why: SEO · rich-result eligibility · rating stability

Don’t emit the aggregateRating JSON-LD block until a product has at least 3 approved reviews. Below that, render the page with no schema and the empty-state message.

Two reasons this matters: one 1-star review pulls the AggregateRating to 1.0 across the entire product, and Google generally won’t render the star result below 3 reviews anyway. So emitting the schema early gives you the worst of both worlds — volatile ratings, no SERP upside.

2 Manual review entry in the admin panel

Why: Seed-phase volume · bridge to magic-link flow

Admin needs a way to log reviews that come in via phone, text, or email during the seed phase. Each entry must be tied to a real order ID and tagged as “Verified buyer (manually entered)”. Same approve / reject flow, same audit trail — just a different submission channel.

Without this, the page sits at “No reviews yet” until the magic-link flow has been running for 6+ months. The seed phase is what bridges that gap.

3 Token rules — explicit specification

Why: Abuse prevention · data integrity

The original spec mentions tokens but doesn’t pin down the rules. Lock these four in:

  • Expiry window: 60 days from send. Token dies after that.
  • One-use only: token invalidates the moment a review is submitted against it.
  • Status gate: token only works if the underlying order is marked Delivered in the system — not Shipped, not Ordered.
  • Rate limit: max 3 submission attempts per token per hour. Protects against scripted abuse if a token ever leaks.

4 Email cadence definition

Why: Response rate · not annoying buyers

The original spec stops at the first email. The full cadence needs to be:

  • +7 days post-delivery: initial review request.
  • +14 days post-delivery: single reminder if no submission.
  • After that: stop. No third email.

Two-touch is industry standard for B2B post-purchase. Three or more pushes response rate down and complaint rate up.

5 Predefined rejection reason taxonomy

Why: ACCC compliance · audit defensibility

The moderation queue must force the moderator to pick a rejection reason from a closed list:

  • PII / personal info
  • Libel or defamatory
  • Off-topic
  • Suspected fake
  • Profanity
  • Other (free text required)

This is the single biggest piece of evidence that rejections aren’t rating-based. If the ACCC ever queries the moderation log, the taxonomy is what proves Chem Connect wasn’t gating low ratings.

6 Per-product “reviews disabled” flag

Why: Custom orders · one-off SKUs

A single checkbox on the product admin page that prevents the post-delivery email from firing for that SKU. Needed for custom blends, one-off bulk orders, and any product where a public review wouldn’t make sense.

7 Photo upload on the submit form

Why: Visual social proof · AI search signal

Optional, max 3 images, max 5MB each. Display below the review text on the product page, with a lightbox click-through.

Cement-coated trucks pre/post Green Acid Replacement is genuinely valuable visual proof — it’s also exactly the kind of content AI search engines disproportionately weight when deciding what to cite.

8 Review removal endpoint for Privacy Act requests

Why: Privacy Act 1988 compliance

If a customer asks for their review to be removed (they can, under the Privacy Act 1988), admin needs a one-click hard-delete that:

  • Wipes the review record.
  • Wipes the underlying token.
  • Logs the deletion (moderator + timestamp + reason “Privacy Act request”) in the audit trail.

Hard-delete — not soft-delete — is the right behaviour for a Privacy Act request. The audit log entry is the proof that the deletion happened.

3. Summary — what to send him

The ask: “Before you start scoping the Customer Reviews build, please add the eight items in §1 to the spec, and come back with answers to the four questions in §2 so we can size the work properly. Most of the eight are small additions to existing flows — the photo upload (item 7) is the only one that’s materially separate work.”

Items 1, 3, 4, and 5 are small spec additions — effectively zero extra build time, just clearer rules. Items 2, 6, and 8 are admin-panel surface area. Item 7 (photo upload) is the only one with meaningful image-handling, storage, and lightbox work attached.
