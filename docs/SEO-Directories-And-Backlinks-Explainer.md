# Australian directories & backlinks — what they are and how to do them

A plain-English explainer for the two SEO operational items that aren't code-implementable. Designed to be skimmed by someone who's never done SEO before.

---

## Part 1 — Why backlinks matter (the 30-second version)

Google ranks websites partly by **how many other reputable websites link to them**. A link from a respected industry site is essentially a vote of confidence — Google interprets it as "this site knows what it's talking about." More high-quality votes = higher rankings.

There are two ways to earn those votes:

1. **Directories** — listing your business on standard Australian business directories. Foundational, mostly free, takes ~3-4 hours total to do once. Every B2B site needs these.
2. **Editorial / earned links** — articles, mentions, guest posts on industry publications. Higher quality than directories but takes more effort.

Both feed the same SEO engine. Directories first because they're quick and necessary; editorial links over time because they're what actually moves rankings long-term.

## Part 2 — The directories playbook

### Why each directory matters

Every Australian directory listing does **three** things at once:

1. **Backlink** — adds one more "vote" to Chem Connect's authority score.
2. **NAP consistency** — the business's Name, Address, Phone matches across the web. Google rewards consistency; mismatches confuse it and hurt local SEO.
3. **Direct traffic** — buyers actually search these directories. Most won't outrank Google but they'll send a trickle of qualified leads.

### The list, in priority order

Each one needs the **same** Name + Address + Phone (NAP) data. If you list one warehouse on Yellow Pages and a slightly different version on True Local, Google reads it as "two different businesses" — which kills your local SEO.

Use this NAP block on every listing:

```
Name:    Chem Connect (operated by Concrete & Quarry Vending Systems Pty Ltd)
Email:   support@chemconnect.com.au
Website: https://www.cqvs-chemconnect.com.au
ABN:     [client to supply]
Phone:   [client to supply — use the head-office number, not warehouse]
```

For directories that allow multiple locations, list **all 7 active warehouses**:

### Tier 1 — must-do (do these in week 1)

| Directory | URL | Cost | Time | What you get |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Australian Business Register (ABR)** | abr.gov.au | Free | 5 min | Your ABN listing is already public if you have an ABN; just confirm the trading name and contact email are current. The most authoritative AU business signal. |
| **Yellow Pages AU** | yellowpages.com.au | Free / paid tiers | 30 min | Australia's biggest general business directory. Free listing covers basics; paid tiers get you priority placement. |

**Tier 1 total time:** about 90 minutes for all five. Do them in one sitting with the NAP block above.

### Tier 2 — high-value (do these in weeks 2–3)

| Directory | URL | Cost | What you get |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Local chambers of commerce** | each warehouse city has one — Greater Geelong, Greater Dandenong, Brisbane North, etc. | $200–400/year membership | Strong local backlink + actual networking referrals |
| **Industry directories** | australianchemicalsuppliers.com, ChemSupply Distributors, etc. | Free–$500/year | Industry-specific authority — Google weighs these heavily for "chemical supplier" queries |
| **State EPA suppliers lists** | NSW EPA, VIC EPA, QLD DES, WA DWER, SA EPA — most have public licensee / supplier lists | Free | Very high-trust government links. Worth the paperwork. |

### Tier 3 — long-term plays (months 2–3)

| Directory | URL | Why |
|---|---|---|
| **RACI** (Royal Australian Chemical Institute) | raci.org.au | Member directory link is a prestige signal Google trusts |
| **IChemE Australia** | icheme.org | Same — chemical engineering authority |
| **Chemistry Australia** | chemistryaustralia.org.au | Industry peak body — highest-authority backlink available in this niche |

These typically need an actual membership (not free) but the link quality justifies it.

### Common mistakes to avoid

- ❌ **Different phone numbers per directory** — kills NAP consistency. Use the same head-office number everywhere.
- ❌ **Listing every warehouse with a separate ABN** — confuses Google. One ABN, multiple addresses.
- ❌ **Paid directory spam** — there are dozens of pay-to-list "directories" that exist only to charge you. They send no traffic and Google ignores their backlinks. Stick to the list above.
- ❌ **Doing all 30 in one week** — Google's spam filter looks for unnatural backlink growth. Spread the Tier 1 listings over a week, Tier 2 over a month.

## Part 3 — The backlinks playbook

Backlinks beyond directories come from **earning** them. Three reliable ways:

### Strategy 1 — Guest posts on industry publications

**What it is:** You write a useful article (1,000–1,500 words) for an established industry publication. They publish it under your byline with a link back to Chem Connect.

**Why it works:** Google sees a link from a respected site and treats it as a vote of confidence. Buyers reading the publication see your name as an industry expert.

**Target publications for Chem Connect:**

| Publication | Audience | Pitch angle |
|---|---|---|
| Concrete & Quarry News | concrete plant operators | "How acid replacements are reshaping plant cleaning OH&S in 2026" |
| Australian Mining Review | mining procurement | "Cutting freight costs on remote-site chemical supply" |
| Manufacturers' Monthly | factory operators | "Compliance changes coming for industrial chemical buyers" |
| Australian Bulk Handling Review | logistics / freight | "What's actually changed in ADG Code interpretation in 2026" |
| Process Worldwide / ProcessOnline | process engineers | "AUD pricing transparency in B2B chemical sourcing" |

**Process:**

1. Pick one publication per quarter. Read 3-4 of their recent articles to learn their tone.
2. Email the editor with a 3-line pitch: "I'd like to write an article for you on X, here's why your readers will care, here's why I'm qualified."
3. Once accepted, write the article. Include 1–2 links to relevant Chem Connect pages naturally (knowledge hub articles, location pages — never just the homepage).
4. Submit. Publication publishes within ~2-4 weeks usually.

**Time cost:** 4–6 hours per article. Realistic cadence: 1 guest post per quarter.

### Strategy 2 — Supplier / brand backlinks

**What it is:** Each manufacturer Chem Connect stocks gets a dedicated brand page on the site. The manufacturers see this and naturally want to link to it from their own marketing — "Our products are stocked at Chem Connect."

**Why it works:** Manufacturers are well-established sites with their own domain authority. Each one linking back is a strong signal.

**Process:**

1. Build a brand page per manufacturer (`/suppliers/{brand-name}` — already in the SEO plan, scheduled for Phase 4 of the build).
2. Email each manufacturer's marketing contact: "We've built a dedicated page for your products on our marketplace — here's the URL, would you mind linking to it from your distributor list?"
3. Most will. Some will ask for a reciprocal mention; that's fine.

**Time cost:** 30 minutes per manufacturer to email and follow up. Scales with the supplier count.

### Strategy 3 — PR moments and broken-link reclamation

**PR moments** are events you announce publicly that journalists / industry sites cover:

- New warehouse opening
- New supplier onboarding
- Sustainability initiative
- Compliance news (e.g., when ADG Code changes, comment on it as an industry expert)
- Annual market reports / data ("Chem Connect 2026 Industrial Chemicals Pricing Report")

Each press mention earns a backlink and direct traffic.

**Broken-link reclamation** is the unglamorous-but-effective tactic of finding industry pages that link to defunct chemical suppliers (companies that shut down, websites that 404'd) and emailing the page owner: "Hey, your link to X is broken — here's a working alternative on Chem Connect."

Tool to find these: Ahrefs, Semrush, or the free Check My Links Chrome extension. Look at competitor "supplier directories" pages and screen for broken outbound links.

**Time cost:** ~2 hours/month sustained gives you 1–3 reclaimed links per month.

### Realistic 6-month targets

| Source | Month 1 | Month 3 | Month 6 |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tier 1 directory listings | 5 | 5 | 5 |
| Tier 2 directory listings | 0 | 5 | 8 |
| Tier 3 industry directory listings | 0 | 1 | 3 |
| Supplier / brand backlinks | 0 | 4 | 12 |
| Guest posts | 0 | 1 | 2 |
| Reclaimed broken links | 0 | 1 | 4 |
| **Total backlinks** | **5** | **17** | **34** |

For a B2B niche like industrial chemicals, **34 high-quality backlinks at 6 months** is genuinely strong. It's the difference between page 4 and page 1 of Google for the kinds of queries Chem Connect cares about.

## Part 4 — What to NOT do

- ❌ **Buy backlink packages.** Sites that sell "100 backlinks for $100" are linking from spam farms. Google detects them and the penalty (manual action) is hard to recover from.
- ❌ **Comment-spam blogs.** Posting "Great article! Check out chemconnect.com.au" on random blog comments. Worth nothing.
- ❌ **Link exchanges.** "I'll link to you if you link to me" was an SEO trick in 2010. Google now ignores reciprocal links and may penalise excessive ones.
- ❌ **Hire a "guaranteed page-1 SEO" firm.** Anyone guaranteeing rankings is using black-hat tactics that work briefly then collapse.

The only tactics that work over time are the ones described in Parts 2 and 3 above. Slow, deliberate, real.
