Day 7 ---> Send Email: "10% discount - 48 hour offer" Day 14 ---> IF no order placed -> Move to "Cold" tag -> IF order placed -> Move to "Customer" tag, exit sequence

How you build it

6. How Each Feature Works Behind The Scenes

You asked me to explain how all this actually works. Here's a plain-English walkthrough of each feature - what's happening under the hood when you click a button, and the work involved in building it. This isn't a small bolt-on - it's a proper system with many moving parts that all have to be engineered, connected, tested, and maintained.

6.1 Sending one-off email & SMS blasts to everyone or a filtered group

What happens when you click "Send":

  1. The system reads your audience filter (e.g., "everyone in NSW with the tag VIP") and builds the exact recipient list from your contact database
  2. It confirms the final count back to you: "This campaign will reach 342 contacts"
  3. A background worker takes over - it breaks the list into small batches (typically 500 at a time) so the admin UI doesn't freeze while thousands of messages go out
  4. For each batch, it hands the message off to Mailgun (email) or Twilio (SMS) one by one, with safety throttling to avoid triggering spam filters
  5. Every send attempt is logged to the database so you can see exactly what happened with each contact
  6. If any individual send fails (e.g., invalid email), the system records it, flags the contact, and continues with the rest - one bad record never kills the whole campaign
Work involved to build this: Audience query engine, filter builder UI with live preview counts, batching logic, background job system with retry handling, rate-limit protection, per-contact send logging, error recovery flow, campaign status tracking (draft / sending / sent / failed), and an admin UI to view live send progress.

6.2 Scheduling emails & SMS for future dates or recurring sends

What happens when you schedule "Next Tuesday 9 AM":

  1. The campaign is saved as a "scheduled" item with your exact send date & time
  2. A scheduler service runs in the background 24/7 - it wakes up every minute and asks: "Is there any campaign due to send right now?"
  3. When the time arrives, the scheduler automatically flips the campaign to "sending" status and kicks off the exact same blast process from 6.1
  4. For recurring campaigns ("every Monday 9 AM"), once a send completes, the scheduler calculates the next run date and re-queues it - indefinitely, until you pause it
  5. If your server is offline when a scheduled send is due, it catches up automatically when the server comes back - so no campaign ever gets lost
  6. You can edit, pause, or cancel any scheduled campaign right up until the send moment
Work involved to build this: 24/7 scheduler service that never sleeps, timezone-aware scheduling (Australian time), recurring-rule engine (daily / weekly / monthly / custom intervals), missed-send recovery logic, scheduled-campaign calendar view in admin, pause / resume / cancel controls, conflict detection (don't send the same thing twice), and full audit logging.

6.3 Automated drip sequences with branching logic

This is the single most complex piece of the whole module. A drip sequence is a "robot" that follows your contacts around for weeks or months and makes decisions based on their behaviour.

How it works:

  1. You design the flow once in the visual builder - drag-and-drop steps, delays, and branching conditions like "if they opened the email" or "if they didn't reply"
  2. The system stores your flow as a blueprint
  3. When a contact hits the trigger (e.g., "New Lead tag added"), the system creates a personal timeline for that contact and starts walking them through the steps
  4. Every minute, the engine checks: "Which contacts are due for their next step right now?" and executes it - sending the email, waiting the delay, or evaluating the branching condition
  5. At each branching point, the engine looks up whether that contact opened, clicked, replied, or ordered, then routes them down the correct path
  6. Hundreds of contacts can be on different steps of different sequences at the same time - the engine keeps track of each one individually
  7. If a contact unsubscribes or completes a goal mid-sequence (e.g., places an order), they're automatically removed from the flow
Work involved to build this: Visual drag-and-drop flow builder (similar to tools like Zapier), flow-definition data model, per-contact enrollment tracking, a step-execution engine that runs 24/7, branching-condition evaluator (queries Mailgun/Twilio data to check opens, clicks, replies), delay & timing logic with timezone awareness, exit conditions, goal tracking, test-mode simulator, pause / resume controls per sequence, versioning (so updating a sequence doesn't break contacts already inside it), and full execution history per contact. This alone is equivalent to building a mini-Zapier inside ChemConnect.

6.4 Paste Claude-generated HTML directly into campaigns

What happens when you paste HTML:

  1. You paste your HTML into the campaign editor. A live preview window shows exactly how your email will look in Gmail, Outlook, Apple Mail, etc.
  2. Before sending, the system runs your HTML through a safety and deliverability processor that:
    • Scans for errors or broken code
    • Converts modern CSS into the old-style inline CSS that email clients actually understand (many email apps like Outlook don't support modern styling)
    • Automatically rewrites every link to go through Mailgun's click tracker (so you see who clicked what)
    • Injects an invisible open-tracking pixel at the bottom
    • Inserts your unsubscribe link in the footer automatically
    • Adds the CQVS sender identity and physical address required by law
    • Replaces personalisation tags like {{first_name}} with each recipient's actual name
  3. A spam-score checker flags anything that might trigger spam filters (certain words, too many images, bad link ratios) before you send
  4. You can save reusable templates so you don't have to paste fresh HTML every time
Work involved to build this: HTML editor with syntax highlighting, live multi-client email preview (Gmail / Outlook / mobile / dark mode), CSS inlining engine, link-rewriting system, tracking-pixel injection, personalisation-tag parser and replacement, spam-score analysis, template library with save / load / duplicate, version history, and an HTML sanitiser to block malicious code.

6.5 Receiving SMS replies in a built-in inbox

This is one of the most technically involved parts because it needs to work in real-time.

How it works:

  1. Twilio assigns you a dedicated business phone number that customers will see when they receive your SMS
  2. I configure Twilio to notify ChemConnect the instant anyone replies to that number
  3. When Sarah texts back "Yes I'm interested", within a second or two:
    • Twilio hands the message to ChemConnect
    • ChemConnect matches the phone number to Sarah's contact record
    • The message is saved into a conversation thread tied to her profile
    • A red-dot notification appears in your admin inbox
  4. You open the inbox and see a WhatsApp-style conversation view
  5. When you type a reply and hit send, ChemConnect hands your message to Twilio, which delivers it to Sarah's phone
  6. Every message (inbound and outbound) is permanently logged and attached to her contact profile
  7. Unknown numbers (people not in your contact list) automatically create a new contact record so you never lose a lead
  8. Opt-out keywords like "STOP" or "UNSUBSCRIBE" are detected automatically - the contact is removed from all future campaigns without you having to do anything (legally required)
Work involved to build this: Real-time webhook receiver with security verification, phone-number matching to contacts, conversation-thread data model, WhatsApp-style inbox UI with unread indicators, real-time notifications in admin, outbound reply system, auto-creation of new contacts from unknown numbers, STOP / UNSUBSCRIBE keyword detection, conversation search and filtering, thread assignment (if multiple team members), and full audit logging of every inbound and outbound message.

6.6 Tracking who opened, clicked, replied, or unsubscribed

Every email has hundreds of tiny events happening behind the scenes. Here's what the system is listening for:

EventWhat It MeansTracked By
DeliveredThe email successfully reached the recipient's mail serverMailgun -> ChemConnect
OpenedThe recipient actually opened the email (detected via an invisible tracking pixel)Mailgun -> ChemConnect
ClickedThey clicked a specific link - I record which link and whenMailgun -> ChemConnect
BouncedInvalid email address - the contact is auto-flagged so you don't keep sending to a dead addressMailgun -> ChemConnect
UnsubscribedThey clicked the unsubscribe link - auto-excluded from all future sendsMailgun -> ChemConnect
Spam complaintThey marked it as spam - flagged for review, harms your sender reputationMailgun -> ChemConnect
SMS delivered / failedThe text message reached the phone or failedTwilio -> ChemConnect
SMS repliedThe contact texted back (see 6.5)Twilio -> ChemConnect

How it flows:

  1. Every time any of the above happens, Mailgun or Twilio instantly notifies ChemConnect over a secure, verified connection
  2. ChemConnect records the event, the contact, the campaign, the timestamp, and any extra data (which link, which device, etc.)
  3. The analytics dashboard and per-campaign reports calculate live metrics from this data - open rate, click rate, bounce rate, best-performing links, active-hour heatmaps, and more
  4. This data also feeds the sequence engine (6.3) so it knows whether to branch a contact down the "opened" path or the "didn't open" path
Work involved to build this: Secure webhook endpoints for Mailgun and Twilio with signature verification, event database and indexing, real-time event processing pipeline, per-campaign analytics queries, dashboard with multiple chart types and date filtering, per-contact activity timeline, CSV export of campaign reports, suppression list management (bounces, unsubs, spam complaints auto-excluded from future sends), and a sender-reputation monitor.

6.7 The Foundation Layer (Not Visible, But Essential)

On top of the features above, there's a foundation layer of work that has to be built just to hold everything together. None of it is "fancy" but all of it is required:

  • Database design - new tables for contacts, tags, segments, campaigns, sequences, enrollments, events, SMS messages, templates, suppression lists, and audit logs (roughly 12-15 new database tables)
  • Secure API integration with Mailgun and Twilio, including credential management, webhook signature verification, and error handling for when those services go down
  • Role-based permissions - Marketing Admin vs Marketing Editor access control
  • GDPR & Australian Spam Act compliance - consent tracking, unsubscribe handling, data export on request, retention policies
  • CSV import engine - column mapping, duplicate detection, validation, error reporting
  • Auto-sync with ChemConnect customer table - so existing customers appear in contacts automatically
  • Testing mode - safe sandbox to test campaigns without sending real emails
  • Performance & scalability - the system needs to handle a 2,000-contact blast without slowing the rest of ChemConnect down
  • Monitoring & alerts - so I know if Mailgun is rate-limiting, if a sequence is stuck, or if a scheduled send fails
  • Documentation & admin help screens - so you and your team can use it without training
The honest picture: The UI you see (four tabs and some buttons) is the visible 20% of the work. The other 80% is the invisible scheduling engines, event pipelines, compliance handling, and integrations that make everything reliable. This module is a significant build - comparable in scope to the MacShip + Xero + Lead Times integration I'm wrapping up now, and arguably larger because it touches more of the platform.

7. Deliverability - Making Sure You Don't Land in Spam

Since you're sending from jonny@cqvs.com.au and your DNS is at GoDaddy, I'll configure the following once - and then every email you send will be properly authenticated.

DNS RecordWhat It Does
SPFTells Gmail/Outlook "Mailgun is allowed to send emails for cqvs.com.au"
DKIMCryptographic signature that proves the email really came from you (not a spammer pretending)
DMARCPolicy that tells mail providers what to do if authentication fails (reject fake emails)

Plus, every email will automatically include:

  • Unsubscribe link in the footer (legally required under Australian Spam Act 2003)
  • CQVS sender identity with your business address
  • One-click unsubscribe - clicking it automatically removes them from your list, no admin action needed
I'll need a 5-minute screen-share on GoDaddy to add the SPF/DKIM/DMARC records to cqvs.com.au. After that, deliverability is set up permanently.
